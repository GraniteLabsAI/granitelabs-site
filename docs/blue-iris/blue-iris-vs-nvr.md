## ⚖️ Core Difference

Blue Iris:
- Software platform running on a PC  
- Fully configurable  
- Hardware independent  

NVR:
- Locked hardware appliance  
- Limited configuration  
- Vendor-dependent  

---

## ⚡ Performance

Blue Iris:
- Depends on CPU/GPU  
- Scales with hardware  
- Can support many cameras  

NVR:
- Fixed performance  
- Limited channels  
- Cannot upgrade  

---

## 🤖 AI Detection

Blue Iris:
- ONNX / CodeProject AI  
- Custom models  
- Adjustable confidence  

NVR:
- Basic detection  
- Limited tuning  
- Often unreliable  

---

## 🔧 Flexibility

Blue Iris:
- Full control  
- Multi-brand cameras  
- Custom workflows  

NVR:
- Vendor ecosystem  
- Limited integrations  
- Fixed features  

---

## 💾 Storage

Blue Iris:
- Full control of storage  
- Custom retention  
- Multiple drives  

NVR:
- Fixed drive bays  
- Limited control  
- Basic retention  

---

## 💰 Cost

Blue Iris:
- Software license + PC  
- Higher upfront flexibility  

NVR:
- Lower upfront cost  
- Limited long-term value  

---

## ⚠️ Real-World Limitations

Blue Iris:
- Requires setup knowledge  
- Needs tuning  

NVR:
- Easy to start  
- Hard to improve  

---

## 🧠 When to Choose Blue Iris

- You want full control  
- You need AI accuracy  
- You plan to scale  
- You want long-term flexibility  

---

## 🧠 When to Choose an NVR

- You want plug-and-play  
- You have a small system  
- You do not want to configure

## 🧠 Real-World Recommendation

Choose Blue Iris if:

- You want full control over your system  
- You plan to scale beyond 4–8 cameras  
- You need reliable AI detection  
- You want long-term flexibility  

Choose an NVR if:

- You want a simple plug-and-play setup  
- You are running a small system  
- You do not plan to customize or expand  

---

## ✅ Result

Blue Iris provides significantly more control, flexibility, and long-term value, while NVR systems provide simplicity but limited capability.
